# Vokda — Vision

## What Vokda Is

**Vokda is the central destination for everything TTS.**

It's the place you go when you need a voice — and the place you come back to when you want to know what's happening in the TTS world.

Two things, unified:

1. **Voice Discovery** — find, listen, and collect TTS voices across every major provider and open-source model. Think Pinterest for voices: browse, play, pin, organize, export.

2. **Industry Hub** — track new model releases, read reviews, follow TTS news, and get guidance on which voice fits which job.

Most tools solve one of these. Vokda is the only place that does both.

---

## The Core Loop

```
Discover → Listen → Pin → Organize → Export
```

A user lands on Vokda looking for a narration voice. They browse the grid, play samples from AWS Polly, ElevenLabs, and Kokoro. They pin three candidates to a "Sci-Fi Audiobook" collection. They export the collection as a Voice Pack and plug it into their pipeline.

They come back next week because Vokda sent them a digest: Cartesia dropped a new model. They listen, add it to their collection.

That's the flywheel.

---

## Why This Matters

The TTS landscape is exploding:
- Cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google, OpenAI, ElevenLabs) release new voices constantly
- Open-source models (Kokoro, Qwen3, XTTS, Fish Audio) are getting production-quality fast
- Cartesia, PlayHT, Hume, and others are building entirely new paradigms (ultra-low latency, emotional, real-time)
- Nobody is tracking this systematically in one place

Creators, developers, and studios need a trusted source to stay current and make confident voice choices. Vokda becomes that source.

---

## Who It's For

**Primary:** Developers and engineers building voice-powered apps, agents, and pipelines. They need to compare voices across providers, evaluate quality, and select the right fit.

**Secondary:** Content creators — podcasters, audiobook producers, video makers — who need the right voice for a project and want to audition options before committing.

**Tertiary:** TTS enthusiasts, researchers, and industry watchers who want a curated feed of what's happening in the space.

---

## Principles

1. **Listen-first.** Audio is the product. The UI gets out of the way.
2. **Opinionated curation.** Not every voice makes it in. Quality and diversity matter.
3. **Provider-agnostic.** Cloud, open-source, self-hosted — Vokda covers the full landscape without favoring any one provider.
4. **Discoverable without an account.** The catalog is open. Saving and exporting requires sign-in.
5. **Built for repeat visits.** News, model tracking, and digest features bring people back.
6. **Practical guidance.** "Which voice for animation dubbing?" should have an answer.
